ABOUT ME

My key qualities

Skilled Programmer

I'm fluent with many coding languages, including Python, C, C++, C#, Java, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. I'm familiar with making games using Android Studio, Unity, and Unreal Engine. I have some experience with digital art, animation, and 3D modeling/rigging. I'm open to learn new things to accomplish my goals. I've demonstrated my will to learn throughout my time pursuing my passions, and I'll continue to do so.

Passionate

I've been a gamer since I was a kid, and I'm excited to join the gaming industry to make games that make an impact. I've made several mobile games in college and some Unity games in my free time. I'm currently working on an original FPS game using Unreal Engine. I've also pursued art as a hobby of mine, so I have some experience with digital art, animation, and 3D modeling/rigging in case it comes in handy.

Creative Designer

I'm an active gamer, so I'm in touch with what the players would like in terms of game design. With each project, I set out to create a gem of a game through creative game mechanics, level design, and innovative features.

Team-Oriented

While I'm primarily focused on my own tasks, I'm also open to help others with any issues or give feedback for concerns they might have on a project. A great game begins with a great team, so I value a relaxed, yet focused, environment where team members can work together at peak performance.

I know games.

I've played a wide variety of games, including the Dark Souls series, Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice, Genshin Impact, League of Legends, VALORANT, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, Overwatch, Fate/Grand Order, Kingdom Come: Deliverance, and Skullgirls. In almost any game I've played, I've tried to explore all the possibilities in terms of dialogue options, game mechanics, achievements, etc. In the process, I learn a lot about how the games were designed, what makes the games great, and what each game could do to improve. I take this experience into consideration when making my games to ensure that the final product will be just as enjoyable for both the player and the developer.
